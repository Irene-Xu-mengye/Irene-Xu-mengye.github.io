PhD Application Resources


Recruitment/Diversity Programs

Some universities have programs for applicants coming from diverse backgrounds. You usually need to apply for them and they would do their best to make the process fair regardless of where you are in your journey. Even if you don’t apply for these programs, their websites usually have information from previous years’ workshops. Personally I attended the first three and they have been helpful.
One particular advantage of university programs is to hear from their own faculty and sometimes even get the chance to talk to them and/or receive feedback from them. If you are interested in a particular faculty or a particular program, definitely try apply to their recruitment program if you can!

  • Harvard: Prospective Ph.D. & RA Event in Psychology (PPREP)
  • University of North Carolina: Diversifying Psychology Weekend (DPW)
  • Rutgers: Diversifying Psychology Program
  • University of Minnesota: Diversity in Psychology Program
  • University of Michigan: STAR Scholars Program
  • Stanford: Paths to PhD
  • University of Washington: A Sneak Peek for Graduate School
  • Stony Brook: Weekly Psychology Graduate School Application Cafe

Non-Profits/Student-Led Programs

Many of the following programs offer one-to-one mentorship, but even if you are not able to sign up for it, go check out their resources pages. They try to make as many resources available to all.

  • Project SHORT matches applicants with mentors in a related field to provide one-to-one mentoring. “One of the goals of Project SHORT is to offer pro-bono mentoring for individuals from diverse and underrepresented populations in STEM.”
  • Application Statement Feedback Program (ASFP): “Our goal is to provide fast, constructive feedback and editing support for the research/personal statements of PhD applicants in psychology, with an emphasis on underrepresented minorities and/or those who are not ‘in the know.’”
  • Psychin’Out has a resource hub of application materials and slack channels for people with different background and are at different stages of their careers.
  • NextGen Psych Scholars Program (NPSP) is a virtual graduate student-led mentorship program for underrepresented (e.g., BIPOC, low-income, first-gen, LGBTQIA+) undergraduates and post-baccalaureates interested in applying to psychology PhD programs.
  • Getting into Grad School is a program for applicants who plan to apply for Clinical Psychology programs in the current cycle. They provide monthly meetings, feedback on application materials and interview practice.

Diversity Statement

Diversity Statement is a relatively new application material. A few schools require them and they usually have specific prompts. There hasn’t been many resources on how to write a good diversity statement as a psychology PhD applicant yet. If you know of one, please let me know! (form at the end of the page).
