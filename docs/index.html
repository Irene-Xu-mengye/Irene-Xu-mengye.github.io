About

Thank you for visiting my website. I am Irene Xu (pronunciation) and I use they/them pronouns. I am an incoming PhD student in the Clinical Psychology Program at the University of Notre Dame. I work with Dr. Brooke Ammerman at the Affect, Suicide, Self-Injury, and Social Triggers (ASSIST) Lab and the Notre Dame Suicide Prevention Initiative - Research, Intervention, and Training (SPIRIT).

Research

Some of the questions that my research tries to answer are:

  • Why do some individuals engage in suicidal behaviors while some others are able to stop themselves?
  • Does temporal dissociation serve a protective function that stop the transition from ideation to attempt?
  • What can we learn from the extremely high rate of SITB in the gender diverse population?

To answer these questions, I employ a wide range of methods, including cross-sectional, longitudinal, and real-time monitoring study designs. Given the complex nature of SITBs, I am trying to develop a statistical expertise in analyzing a variety of data. See my recent projects here.
