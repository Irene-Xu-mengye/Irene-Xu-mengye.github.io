Finding Suicide Labs


To reduce barriers for students passionate about suicide research, especially those from underrepresented backgrounds, there has been efforts to put together resources on finding researchers, research labs, and organizations in the suicide and self-injury field.

  • Psychin’Out has a list of PhD Advisor Directory organized by research interests including suicide and self-injury.

  • Dr. Blaire Ehret has a list on suicide faculty accepting students in particular years.

  • Dr. Abby Ridge-Anderson has a website on Resources For Suicide Researchers that involve suicide related networking, organizations, conferences, training, measures, datasets, etc.

  • To learn about current research or present your work at early-career-friendly conferences, there are two free virtual conferences that I’ve had good experience with: Suicide Research Symposium (SRS) and Youth Suicide Research Consortium (YSRC).

Since the existing lists are organized by particular application cycles, I tried to compile a list of faculty and researchers specializing in the field of suicide and self injury and are able to admit students or RAs.

This list has columns indicating

  1. if the researcher can accept grad students or RAs, and
  2. if they plan to accept grad student(s) in the current cycle.

The list includes not just researchers in the field of clinical psychology, but also the field of psychiatry, neuroscience, social work, public health, etc. While clinical psychology is where most of the research resides in, I believe that suicide prevention benefits from interdisciplinary collaborations and want to show early career scholars that there are many potential career paths where you can contribute to suicide research.
